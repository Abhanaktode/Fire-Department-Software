Fire Safety Tips & Guidelines

Essential information to keep you and your family safe

Fire Safety Week 2024

Join us in promoting fire safety awareness. Learn, practice, and share these life-saving tips with your community.

Smoke Detectors

Install and maintain smoke alarms on every level of your home, inside bedrooms, and outside sleeping areas.

Learn More

Escape Plan

Create and practice a home fire escape plan with two ways out of every room and a meeting point outside.

Learn More

Kitchen Safety

Never leave cooking unattended. Keep flammable items away from the stove and have a fire extinguisher nearby.

Learn More

Fire Extinguishers

Keep fire extinguishers in key locations and know how to use them. Remember: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep (PASS).

Learn More

Electrical Safety

Avoid overloading outlets, replace damaged cords, and never run cords under rugs or furniture.

Learn More

Candles & Open Flames

Keep candles at least 12 inches from flammable items. Never leave them burning unattended or while sleeping.

Learn More

Exit Routes

Know all exit routes from your workplace. Keep exits clear and never block fire doors or emergency equipment.

Learn More

Fire Drills

Participate in all fire drills. Take them seriously and use them as practice for a real emergency.

Learn More

Report Hazards

Report fire hazards immediately to management. This includes blocked exits, damaged equipment, or unsafe practices.

Learn More

Get Out & Stay Out

If there's a fire, get out immediately. Never go back inside a burning building for any reason.

Learn More

Crawl Low Under Smoke

Smoke rises, so stay low to the ground where the air is clearer. Crawl on your hands and knees to escape.

Learn More

Check Doors First

Before opening a door, feel it with the back of your hand. If it's hot, use another exit route.

Learn More

Test Smoke Alarms

Test your smoke alarms monthly and replace batteries at least once a year. Replace the entire unit every 10 years.

Learn More

Fire Sprinklers

Fire sprinklers can control or extinguish a fire in less time than it takes for firefighters to arrive.

Learn More

Teach Kids About Fire

Teach children that fire is a tool, not a toy. Keep matches and lighters out of reach and sight.

Learn More

Stop, Drop & Roll

Teach children to stop, drop, and roll if their clothes catch fire. Practice this with them regularly.

Learn More
